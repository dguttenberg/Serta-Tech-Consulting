Creative Production
Technology Consulting
with Serta.

A proposed method for closing a common gap between creative agencies and their in-house counterparts that allows agency thinking to easily move into execution across a brand ecosystem.

·
The opportunity

Concept lands.
Execution drifts.

A common gap between creative agencies and the in-house teams responsible for scaling their work. Serta has named this themselves, and there's a real opening to fix it.

How it works today

Hero in. Drift out.

Doner produces big ideas and hero assets. Serta's in-house team executes the day-to-day churn including seasonal sales, site content, and retailer work.

What sits between is a campaign concept, hero executions, a high-level style guide, and final files.

The in-house team struggles to convert the heart of the concept into something literal that they can reproduce at scale. They also have access to high-end technology through Adobe that hasn't been activated to deliver true automation capabilities.

How this initiative changes things

A consultative layer that closes the loop.

Doner can play a consultative role that supports Serta getting the best they can out of their technology stack.

As that process is defined, it will unveil the true inputs the in-house team needs to successfully scale Doner's conceptual work.

·
The work

How the work would happen.

Two workstreams, sequenced. Tap a card to expand the detail.

01
Production tech and talent consult.

Understand how the in-house team works before we change anything.

Why this comes first

You can't design a translation system without understanding what's on the receiving end. Serta has significant Adobe investment but isn't getting the full return. The friction is partly tooling and methodology, partly capability of the team using them, and partly the structure of the work coming in.

What we'd do
  • Spend time with the in-house team to understand how they work, where they get stuck, and what they need to be successful
  • Look at how their Adobe investment is currently deployed versus where it could be doing more
  • Recommend a methodology and operating approach that fits their team, their tools, and the work they're responsible for
  • Identify capability gaps that training, restructuring, or sequencing could close
  • Surface the moments where Adobe services hours could implement what we recommend
Why us, not Adobe

Adobe will sell them tools and do some degree of configuration. We can act as a translator layer between Serta's brand, business, and team. We know the work and the business, and we can recommend an approach that's grounded in what Serta is actually trying to make.

What we don't do

Build anything without incremental scope. They may have Adobe hours that they can utilize, and we'd be available to support as needed.

02
Creative-to-production translation system.

Augment what Doner hands off so the in-house team has more to work with, not less to figure out.

The shift

Today's deliverable does what it's supposed to do for a hero campaign. It doesn't do enough for a team responsible for blowing the idea out across hundreds of touchpoints. We add a layer that travels with the work and gives them what they need to extend it well.

What we'd add
  • Encoded design and creative intent that reinforces and extends the style guide
  • Decision trees for how the system extends to formats and contexts we didn't make ourselves
  • Modular templates built for the Adobe ecosystem they actually use

More to come once Workstream 01 tells us what's actually needed.

Why this only works after Workstream 01

The translation layer has to fit the production environment. Designing it without seeing how their team works and what their stack supports produces another deck that looks great and dies on contact.

The upside

What this opens up.

Beyond the scope itself, this initiative creates four meaningful outcomes for the agency.

01 / Reach

Larger share of brand ecosystem.

Today we own hero work and campaign social. This positions us as the strategic operator across more of how the brand actually shows up in market.

02 / Work

More high-value work.

A better system surfaces more places where Doner's creative thinking is the right answer, not just the in-house team's bandwidth.

03 / Partnership

Adobe partnership leverage.

Recommendations naturally surface upsell paths. Stagwell's Adobe relationship turns those into commission.

04 / Position

Defensible position.

Once we're advising on the operational layer, we're harder to displace and we're shaping the brief, not just answering it.
